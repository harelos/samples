Spec strategy teardown

Where you are today

stonehengehealth.com sells 15+ 'science-backed' supplements with branded ingredients, 'Expect More From Your Supplements' positioning, Subscribe & Save up to 45%, named video testimonials, review counts, advisory board and media mentions.

The gaps I'd tackle first

  • Homepage overwhelms with 15+ SKUs and no 'start here' path for first-time buyers
  • Value prop is undifferentiated - quality/third-party-testing claims competitors also make
  • Clinical results footnoted without methodology, weakening credibility for skeptics
  • Site is built for browsing, not persuasion - little narrative, no visible FAQ/guarantee above the fold

How I'd fix it

  • Introduce a guided 'find your product' quiz/flow to route first-time buyers
  • Develop a distinctive brand platform beyond 'quality' (a proprietary formulation or outcome promise)
  • Build creative that leads with quantified, story-driven proof rather than SKU grids
  • Add objection-handling modules (guarantee, side-effects, methodology) into the creative system
  • Establish a performance-creative testing cadence so brand work is validated against CAC/ROAS
