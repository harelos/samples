Spec strategy teardown

Where you are today

OpenAI is early in building and positioning an advertising offering. The brand carries enormous reach and a novel, high-intent surface (ChatGPT), but the advertiser-facing narrative, trust story, and performance proof are nascent versus incumbents.

The gaps I'd tackle first

  • The advertiser value proposition (why advertise here, what outcomes, how measured) is not yet a sharp, performance-led narrative advertisers and agencies can act on
  • Brand-safety and user-trust concerns around ads in an AI assistant are a live objection with no clear, proactive answer for buyers
  • Differentiation vs Meta and Google isn't yet framed around the unique intent/context signal ChatGPT has - it risks sounding like another inventory source
  • Advertiser education and onboarding (formats, measurement, best practices) is thin for a market that will need heavy hand-holding early

How I'd fix it

  • Build a performance-led advertiser narrative grounded in outcomes (intent, context, measured ROAS) rather than reach - written by someone who has bought media and knows what advertisers actually decide on
  • Get ahead of the brand-safety/trust objection with a clear, proactive story and controls, so it's an asset instead of a blocker
  • Frame differentiation around the unique high-intent, conversational context signal, with concrete before/after use cases vs Meta/Google
  • Stand up advertiser education and enablement (formats, measurement, playbooks) to lower the activation barrier for early advertisers and agencies
