Spec strategy teardown

Where you are today

Stonehenge relies on product-page reviews, named video testimonials and paid media; organic social and editorial authority are thin relative to the science-forward positioning it claims.

The gaps I'd tackle first

  • Little educational/authority content above the fold - the 'science-backed' claim isn't supported by a visible content library
  • No clear owned-audience or newsletter engine to reduce paid dependence
  • Testimonials lack specific, story-driven claims that platform-native content thrives on
  • FTC-compliance risk grows sharply at 20-25 pieces/week without a strong editorial system

How I'd fix it

  • Stand up a hub-and-spoke content system (pillar authority pieces + platform-native cutdowns) to hit volume without diluting quality
  • Launch a lifecycle-fed newsletter to build an owned audience and lower blended CAC
  • Codify an avatar-language + claims-compliance style guide so writers scale voice and stay FTC-safe
  • Repurpose customer outcome stories into short-form proof content for organic and paid
  • Set organic-reach and assisted-conversion KPIs so content ties to revenue, not impressions
