The most intimate product in your home, and the one you have never read the label on

You scrutinize every ingredient that goes on your face. The product that touches far more sensitive skin than that usually gets no scrutiny at all.
By Harel FilibaUpdated July 20265 min read
Image direction
Soft, tasteful editorial still life. Warm morning light on a nightstand, a folded linen, a glass of water, a single unlabeled amber bottle just out of focus. Calm, adult, discreet. Nothing explicit, no branding.

Here is a strange little inconsistency almost everyone lives with. You read labels now. You turned over the moisturizer and frowned at the fifth ingredient. You have opinions about what touches your face. And then there is one product, used on some of the most sensitive skin on the body, that most people have genuinely never read the back of even once.

Personal lubricant is, for a lot of couples, one of the most intimate things in the house. And it is also, quietly, one of the least examined. It gets grabbed off a drugstore shelf on brand recognition, kept in a drawer, and never questioned, as if it were exempt from the same scrutiny we now give a hand soap.

So flip one over sometime. A surprising number are built on ingredients you would never knowingly choose for delicate skin. Glycerin. Petroleum-derived compounds. Preservatives and warming agents chosen for shelf life and sensation, not for the fact that they are being applied to the most absorbent tissue on the body.

We would never put those ingredients on a newborn. And yet we apply them, without a glance, somewhere far more sensitive than a cheek.

And then people blame themselves for the aftermath. The irritation. The discomfort that arrives later. The quiet assumption that their body is simply too sensitive, too difficult, that something is wrong with them. When very often the thing that was wrong was sitting in the drawer the whole time, an ingredient list nobody thought to read.

Which is the small, almost obvious idea a founder could not let go of. If the skin here is this sensitive, and this absorbent, then what it is made of is not a footnote. It is the entire point. So why is nearly the whole category built as though the ingredients are the last thing that matters?

The answer they landed on started with a single, almost old-fashioned ingredient, one you already trust enough to eat, and where they took it from there is where this stops being about the drawer.
